What are GANs?

GANs are a class of machine learning frameworks designed by Ian Goodfellow and his colleagues in 2014. Two neural networks contest with each other in a game (in the form of a zero-sum game, where one agent's gain is another agent's loss). Given a training set, this technique learns to generate new data with the same statistics as the training set. For example, a GAN trained on abstract art can generate new paintings of abstract art that look at least superficially authentic to human observers, having many realistic characteristics.
